Sorrel Drink

  • Servings: 12
  • Cook Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 8 hours

As, the holiday season approaches, I am reminded of all of the foods and drinks I enjoy back home, and no family gathering is complete without sorrel. Sorrel is Jamaica's version of a Hibiscus drink, made from the Roselle Hibiscus flower. The flowers from this plant are harvested in the Caribbean around November, so it has become a staple for Jamaican holiday gatherings. But the blossom's petals can be dried and stored to be enjoyed year-round. The wine-colored drink is refreshingly sweet, with a spice infused kick. This recipe is easy to follow, and the best part is, you can adjust it to your own personal tastes. This drink can be stored in the fridge, or in a cool dark place for up to a year. If you're planning to serve this drink at your next gathering, it is reccomended to make it a couple days in advance. It'll get better with age! Gather the ingredients listed below, and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 8 oz Dried Sorrel

    Dried sorrel looks like dark red flower petals, and can be purchased at many grocery stores ready for use.

  • Crushed Ginger

    Fresh ginger is always best, but minced ginger, juice concentrate or powder are good substitutes.

  • Rum

    To make this drink authentically Jamaican, use Wray & Nephew brand rum. Rum is mainly used for it's flavor here, but the ingredient is optional, if you don't drink alcohol.

  • Pimento or Allspice

    If you like your drink with a spicy kick, make sure to include this ingredient.

  • Sugar

    White or brown sugar are both good options.

  • Lemon Juice

    Sorrel on its own has a tangy-fruit profile, which is complimented by adding lemon.

Instructions

  • Step 1

    Add sorrel, ginger and spices (optional) to a pot with about 1 gallon of water. Place over high heat and allow it to come to a boil, then turn down the heat and let it boil for 5-10 minutes.

  • Step 2

    Remove the pot from the heat and allow it to cool. Transfer the drink into a covered container, and leave it to steep overnight so that all the flavors are infused.

  • Step 3

    The next day, strain the liquid, into a new container, discarding the trash. Then add sugar, lemon juice and rum to taste.

  • Step 4

    Serve cold with ice and enjoy!
